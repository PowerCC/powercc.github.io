# FishFish Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 29, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how FishFish, also known as "鱼鱼" (the "Game"), collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you play the Game on iOS or Android.

This policy is written for the current planned release scope of FishFish. The Game is a single-player, cozy aquarium game. It does not include advertising, in-app purchases, social networking, leaderboards, user-generated content, chat, or location-based gameplay.

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Game progress data

The Game stores your gameplay progress so that your aquarium can be restored when you return. This may include:

- Coin balance and total earned coins
- Fish species, fish visual variants, fish names or identifiers, fish hunger, happiness, position, life stage, and growth progress
- Aquarium water quality, filter status, water-change status, and related timestamps
- Plant inventory, placed decorations, and layout information
- Fish food inventory
- Discovered species, unlocked fish, unlocked decorations, catalog progress, and progression timestamps
- Settings such as language, music preference, music volume, FPS display, and frame-rate mode
- Save timestamps, save version, and cloud-sync metadata needed to compare local and cloud saves

This data is used only to provide the Game's save, restore, cloud sync, conflict resolution, and gameplay features.

### 1.2 Local device data

The Game may store the following locally on your device:

- Encrypted local save files
- Local backup save files
- Settings files
- A locally generated anonymous telemetry identifier used for diagnostics and analytics

The Game does not intentionally collect your real name, email address, phone number, precise location, contacts, photos, microphone recordings, camera data, health data, or payment information.

### 1.3 Cloud save account data

If cloud save is available and enabled by your platform account:

- On iOS, the Game may use iCloud Key-Value Store to sync game progress through the iCloud account configured on your device.
- On Android, the Game may use Google Play Games Services Saved Games to sync game progress through the Google account used with Play Games.

We do not receive your iCloud password or Google password. Authentication and account management are handled by Apple or Google.

Cloud save data contains gameplay progress and save metadata. It is used to restore progress across devices, recover progress after reinstalling the Game, and resolve conflicts between local and cloud saves.

### 1.4 Diagnostics and analytics

The Game includes a Firebase bridge for Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics. If enabled in the final distributed build, Firebase may process information such as:

- Anonymous user identifier generated by the Game
- Platform and locale
- App version, operating system version, device model, and technical diagnostic information provided by Firebase SDKs
- Crash reports, stack traces, non-fatal error reports, timestamps, and related technical logs
- Limited gameplay context used for debugging, such as fish-count bucket, coin-balance bucket, and water-quality bucket
- Sanitized analytics events and parameters, if gameplay events are logged

We use this information to understand whether the Game is stable, diagnose crashes, fix bugs, and improve the player experience.

We do not use Firebase data for third-party advertising, ad targeting, or cross-app tracking.

## 2. Information We Do Not Collect

FishFish does not intentionally collect:

- Precise GPS location
- Contacts
- Photos, videos, or files from your device library
- Microphone or camera data
- Payment card information
- Chat messages or user-generated public posts
- Advertising identifiers for ad targeting
- Data for third-party advertising networks

The current planned release does not include ads, in-app purchases, third-party social features, friend systems, rankings, or multiplayer features.

## 3. How We Use Information

We use information for the following purposes:

- Save and restore your game progress
- Sync progress through iCloud or Google Play Games Services when available
- Detect and resolve save conflicts between local and cloud progress
- Maintain settings such as language and audio preferences
- Provide gameplay systems such as fish growth, feeding, water quality, unlocks, catalog progress, aquarium decoration, and offline progress
- Diagnose crashes, non-fatal errors, and performance or stability issues
- Improve game balance, usability, and reliability
- Comply with platform, legal, security, and fraud-prevention requirements

## 4. Local Storage and Security

The Game stores local progress in an encrypted save file on your device. The current local save format uses AES-256-CBC encryption with a random initialization vector and a backup file for recovery. This is intended to protect normal local save data from casual editing and corruption.

No security method is perfect. Local encryption does not make the save file immune to every form of device compromise, reverse engineering, or platform-level access.

If local save data becomes corrupted, the Game may attempt to load a backup. If both the primary save and backup are unavailable or invalid, the Game may fall back to default starting progress so that the Game can continue running.

## 5. Cloud Saves

### 5.1 iOS iCloud

On iOS, FishFish may use Apple's iCloud Key-Value Store to sync your game save. iCloud sync depends on your device settings, iCloud account status, available iCloud storage, and Apple's services.

iCloud data is handled by Apple under Apple's privacy terms and iCloud settings. You can manage iCloud access through your iOS device settings.

### 5.2 Android Google Play Games Services

On Android, FishFish may use Google Play Games Services Saved Games to sync your game save. This requires Google Play Games Services availability and, depending on your device and account state, may require you to be signed in to a Google account.

Google Play Games Services data is handled by Google under Google's privacy terms and Google account settings. You can manage Play Games and Google account settings through your device and Google account controls.

### 5.3 Cloud conflict handling

If the Game detects that both local and cloud saves exist and differ, it may show a conflict screen with summary information such as save time, fish count, and coin balance. This helps you choose which save to keep. The Game should not silently overwrite newer valid progress when a conflict is detected.

### 5.4 Offline fallback

If cloud sync is unavailable, fails, or the platform account is not signed in, the Game continues to use local save data where possible. Cloud sync failure should not prevent offline play.

Cloud save data may remain in iCloud or Google Play Games Services according to your platform account settings and the retention practices of Apple or Google.

We retain data only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law.
